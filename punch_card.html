    About this job

    Job description

    At LinkedIn, we're passionate about the cutting edge! Join our world-class team and find your ideal job. Explore the latest and greatest technology and flex your creativity. Work and learn from the best. Push your skills higher.  Tackle big problems. Innovate. Create. Punch cards that make a difference in professionals' lives.


    About Software Engineering, Mainframe Infrastructure Division

    The mainframe infrastructure division is the fastest growing segment at the company and one of its most important. We're revolutionizing the way professionals connect, communicate, and share. Our team maintains LinkedIn's horizontal integration platform, acting as a bridge between other teams like Search, Security and Cloud Storage. We bring it all together at LinkedIn. Currently we are integrating the Mobile team and the switchboard operators.

    Our engineers love the flexibility to work across a diverse hardware stack that includes IBM 360, IBM 407, IBM 519 and IBM 1401. Working here allows engineers like you to touch the lives of thousands of users with million dollar computers. Our team is small enough so that you can make an immediate impact, but we're big enough that you can also find your niche and focus on a particular area or technology. The third Friday of every month is casual attire day ... what are you waiting for?

    Apply today and find your dream job in the LinkedIn mainframe infrastructure division!

    Desired Skills and Experience

    You should have a solid foundation in computer science with strong competencies in data structures, algorithms and software design.

    • You have 8+ recent years of experience in Fortran, COBOL or Algol and you are proficient in at least one job control language.
    • Your keyboarding skills are perfect and you thrive in an air conditioned environment driven by waterfall project management.
    • You meet every deadline and you like to work alone.
    • You sequence every card and your deck is backed up to tape at the end of each month.
    • You are patient.
    • You are able to lift and carry large programs between lab rooms.
    • You have a BS, MS or PhD in Computer Science or related discipline.
